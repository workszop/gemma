Kernels

What are Kernels?

Kernels are the low-level GPU programs that do the model's actual math — the matrix multiplications, attention, and normalization behind every token. And how well they're optimized can dramatically speed up inference.

  • WebGPU & WGSL. Each kernel is a WebGPU compute shader, written in WGSL — the language that runs general-purpose math on the GPU — entirely locally in your browser.
  • Agentic Kernel Optimization. Every kernel was generated by AI (in this case, Fable 5, before it was shut down), benchmarked on an Apple M4 Max, and refined through an evolutionary, genetic-style search toward the fastest version.
  • Blazingly Fast. This means we are able to run Gemma 4 E2B at ~250 tokens/sec on an M4 Max, pushing your device to its limits.

Sampling for learning
This in-browser build of Gemma 4 decodes greedily — every step it picks the single highest-probability token (an argmax GPU kernel), so output is deterministic. The sliders below are illustrative: they explain real sampling parameters but don't change this model's replies.
Temperature 1.00

Scales the logits before sampling. Low (→ 0) makes the model confident and repetitive — approaching the greedy decoding used here. High (→ 2) flattens the distribution for more surprising, creative, sometimes incoherent text. 1.0 leaves the probabilities unchanged.

Top-P (nucleus) 0.95

Samples only from the smallest set of tokens whose probabilities sum to P, discarding the unlikely tail. 1.0 considers every token; 0.9 keeps just the most probable ~90% of the mass, trimming odd choices while preserving variety.
